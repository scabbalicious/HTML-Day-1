Thank you for taking the time to visit my small corner of the world. But please don't leave yet. I've got a story to tell. So do stick around for a few minutes.

Who is this guy?

We've already covered my name, but here is a little more about me. I was born and raised in Virginia; not too far from the ocean and not too far from the Chesapeake Bay. I grew up on a steady diet of Thundercats and Nintendo. And Chips Ahoy cookies, which explains why I was a little chubby kid. I wanted to be a doctor, so like a good boy I went through school and college and studied my ass off to be the best doctor out there. And then, one day, I decided I didn't want to be a doctor. So I moved to Japan for a few years to find myself, got tired of that and then moved to Las Vegas to start doing some actual adulting.

Wait...Japan?

Yes. And there is a lot of other stuff that happened too. But don't worry about that now. Stay focused.

So why programming?

Good question. I've always been interested in computers, even though I've never been very good at understanding what's going on under the hood. And I've always been interested in creating things online.

  • Apps
  • Websites
  • Games

But I've never been able to actually build anything. Until now. I am taking the first big, bold step to become an actual programmer.

"Anyone who has never made a mistake has never tried anything new."

--Albert Einstein

Again...why programming?

Programming, when mastered, is a skill that can be done anywhere around the world. And often, at any time. One doesn't need to be stuck in an office, or even in a particular city. One simply needs an internet connection and a device that can produce code. I want to work, but I also want to travel and experience things. And I want to create, and have my creations hopefully live on and be useful to others.

Programming is my means to that end.
